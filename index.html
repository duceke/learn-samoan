Grammar essentials

1. Sentence order: Verb — Subject — Object

Unlike English, Samoan puts the verb first!

'Ua alu le tama.
The boy went.
E fiafia le teine.
The girl is happy.

2. Tense markers

MarkerTenseExampleListen
EPresentE alu au — I go
'UaRecent past'Ua alu — I have gone
SaPastSa alu — I went
O le aFutureO le a alu — I will go

Fa'a Samoa — The Samoan Way

Fa'a Samoa means "the Samoan way" — family first, respect for elders, and service to community.

'Aiga — Extended Family

'Aiga means extended family — the core of Samoan life, including parents, siblings, grandparents, and cousins. Everyone looks after each other!

The Matai System

A matai is the chief or head of a family. Both men and women can be matai.

The 'Ava Ceremony

A special ceremony where a drink made from the pepper plant root is shared to mark important events like welcoming guests or naming a new matai.

Tatau — The Sacred Tattoo

The English word "tattoo" comes from the Samoan tatau ! Tattoos tell the story of a person's family, culture, and identity.

Gagana Samoa — The Language

Gagana Samoa is kept alive through songs, stories, and oral traditions. Samoa Language Week is celebrated every year worldwide!
